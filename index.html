Searching the Web

I have never thought much of a simple component that I interact with daily - whether it's YouTube, Wiki, Google Drive or Twitch - a search bar. Everything now has a search bar and it felt straightforward to use from day one. However, I also realized that I never experienced a mismatch in its design: I am a daily mouse and keyboard user. If you remove one of them, how hard can it get? My intuition told me that it shouldn't be a problem, so today, we will explore how accessible a simple search bar is.

Input checking

The first part of the experiment is to try out the search bars accross different environments. I chose the ones that I use most often, Google Drive, YouTube, and Twitch TV searches. To measure their accessability, I gave all of them a go with using primarely mouse/touchpad, keyboard, or a touchscreen. My main focus points were general component behavior, capabilities, how easy it is to learn and use, and how accessible it felt. You can find the report in the table below
